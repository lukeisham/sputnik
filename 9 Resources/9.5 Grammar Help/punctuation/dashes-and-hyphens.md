# Dashes and Hyphens

Dashes and hyphens are distinct marks with different lengths and purposes.

## Visual Reference

| Mark | Character | Length | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hyphen | - | Shortest | Join words |
| En Dash | – | Medium (width of *n*) | Ranges |
| Em Dash | — | Longest (width of *m*) | Interruption / parenthetical |

## Hyphen (-)

### Join Compound Modifiers Before a Noun
✅ a well-known author
✅ a state-of-the-art facility
✅ a four-year-old child

❌ a well known author *(modifier not hyphenated)*
*(But: The author is well known. — no hyphen when after the noun)*

### Prefixes
✅ self-esteem, ex-president, all-inclusive
✅ preexisting, reenter *(generally no hyphen after common prefixes unless clarity demands it)*

### Numbers
✅ twenty-one through ninety-nine
✅ two-thirds majority

### Avoid Ambiguity
✅ a little-used car *(rarely used)*
❌ a little used car *(a small car that is used)*

✅ She is a foreign-car dealer.
❌ She is a foreign car dealer. *(ambiguous — foreign person or foreign car?)*

## En Dash (–)

### Number Ranges
✅ pages 10–25
✅ 1990–2000
✅ Monday–Friday

### Scores and Connections
✅ The Lakers won 108–95.
✅ the London–Paris train

## Em Dash (—)

### Interruption or Emphasis
✅ She was about to speak — but then she stopped.

### Parenthetical (instead of commas or parentheses)
✅ Three students — Alice, Bob, and Carol — won awards.

### Sudden Break
✅ "I don't know what you —" she began, then fell silent.

### Spacing (Style Dependent)
✅ Word—word *(no spaces — Chicago style)*
✅ Word — word *(spaces — AP style)*

## Common Errors

✅ The Chicago–New York flight. *(en dash for connection)*
❌ The Chicago-New York flight. *(hyphen — too short)*

✅ She arrived — late as usual — and sat down. *(em dash)*
❌ She arrived - late as usual - and sat down. *(hyphen used as dash)*

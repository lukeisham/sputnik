# Commas

Commas are the most frequently used punctuation mark. They separate clauses, items in a list, and set off introductory or parenthetical elements.

## Key Rules

1. **Serial (Oxford) Comma** — Place a comma before the final "and" or "or" in a list of three or more items.
2. **Introductory Phrases** — Use a comma after an introductory word, phrase, or dependent clause.
3. **Coordinating Conjunctions** — Use a comma before *and, but, or, nor, for, so, yet* when joining two independent clauses.
4. **Non-restrictive Clauses** — Set off clauses that add extra (non-essential) information with commas.
5. **Direct Address** — Use commas around the name of the person being spoken to directly.

✅ I bought apples, oranges, and bananas.
❌ I bought apples oranges and bananas.

✅ After the meeting, we went to lunch.
❌ After the meeting we went to lunch.

✅ Let's eat, Grandma!
❌ Let's eat Grandma!

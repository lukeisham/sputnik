# Apostrophes

Apostrophes serve two purposes: showing possession and forming contractions. They are **never** used to form plurals.

## Key Rules

1. **Possession** — Add `'s` for singular nouns; add only an apostrophe for plural nouns ending in *s*.
2. **Contractions** — Use an apostrophe to replace omitted letters (e.g., *don't, it's, can't*).
3. **Its vs It's** — *Its* is possessive (no apostrophe); *it's* is a contraction of "it is" or "it has."
4. **Plurals** — Never use an apostrophe to form regular plurals, including decades and acronyms.

✅ The dog's leash is red. (one dog)
✅ The dogs' park is open. (many dogs)
❌ The dog's are barking. (apostrophe for plural — wrong)

✅ It's raining outside. (it is)
✅ The cat licked its paw. (possessive)
❌ The cat licked it's paw. (wrong)

✅ The 1990s, CDs, FAQs
❌ The 1990's, CD's, FAQ's

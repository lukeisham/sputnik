# Parentheses and Brackets

Parentheses and brackets enclose supplementary, explanatory, or editorial material within text. Each has distinct uses.

## Parentheses ( )

Parentheses enclose material that is **supplementary** — the sentence should still make sense if the contents are removed.

### Uses

1. **Additional Information**
   ✅ The conference (which was held in Paris) was a great success.
   ✅ She finally finished the project (after six long months).

2. **Clarification or Definition**
   ✅ The CMS (Content Management System) was updated.
   ✅ He suffers from apophenia (the tendency to see patterns in random data).

3. **Citations and References**
   ✅ The study showed significant results (Smith, 2024).
   ✅ See the appendix (p. 42) for details.

4. **Abbreviations on First Use**
   ✅ The World Health Organization (WHO) issued new guidelines.

### Punctuation with Parentheses

| Rule | Example |
|---|---|
| Period inside if the whole sentence is parenthetical | We arrived late. (The traffic was terrible.) |
| Period outside if the parenthetical is part of a larger sentence | We arrived late (the traffic was terrible). |
| No comma before the opening parenthesis | ✅ She called (at last) to apologize. ❌ She called, (at last) to apologize. |
| Comma after closing parenthesis if needed | ✅ When I arrived (late, as usual), the meeting had already started. |

## Brackets [ ]

Brackets are used for **editorial insertions** — material added by someone other than the original author.

### 1. Clarifying Quotations
✅ "He [the CEO] announced the merger."
✅ "They arrived in [Paris] that same year." *(replacing "the city" with the actual name)*

### 2. [sic] — Indicating an Error in the Original
When quoting text that contains an error, *[sic]* shows the error is in the original:
✅ She wrote, "The experament [sic] was successful."

### 3. Nested Parentheses
In academic writing, brackets are used inside parentheses:
✅ (The study was replicated three times [see Appendix B].)

## Curly Braces { }

Curly braces are rarely used in prose. Their primary uses are technical:

- **Math/Logic:** set notation — *{1, 2, 3}*
- **Programming:** code blocks
- **Linguistics:** morpheme boundaries — *{un} + {happy}*

## Common Errors

✅ The CEO (who joined last year) announced record profits.
❌ The CEO, (who joined last year) announced record profits. *(comma before paren — wrong)*

✅ He said, "The results were conclusive [and] reproducible."
❌ He said, "The results were conclusive (and) reproducible." *(editorial insertion uses brackets, not parens)*

✅ "The goverment [sic] issued a statement."
❌ "The goverment (sic) issued a statement." *(sic goes in brackets)*

## When NOT to Use Parentheses

Avoid using parentheses for information that should be part of the main sentence:

❌ The manager (who was very angry) fired him.
✅ The manager, who was very angry, fired him. *(commas for non-restrictive clause)*

❌ We need to (a) increase revenue, (b) cut costs, and (c) expand our market.
✅ We need to: (a) increase revenue, (b) cut costs, and (c) expand our market. *(better as a list)*

# Semicolons

A semicolon connects closely related independent clauses without a coordinating conjunction. It is stronger than a comma but weaker than a period.

## Key Rules

1. **Connecting Related Clauses** — Use a semicolon between two independent clauses that are closely related in meaning.
2. **With Conjunctive Adverbs** — When using *however, therefore, moreover, nevertheless*, place a semicolon before the adverb and a comma after.
3. **Complex Lists** — Use semicolons to separate list items that already contain commas.

✅ I have a big test tomorrow; I can't go out tonight.
❌ I have a big test tomorrow, I can't go out tonight.

✅ She studied hard; therefore, she passed the exam.
❌ She studied hard, therefore, she passed the exam.

✅ We visited Paris, France; Rome, Italy; and Berlin, Germany.
❌ We visited Paris, France, Rome, Italy, and Berlin, Germany.

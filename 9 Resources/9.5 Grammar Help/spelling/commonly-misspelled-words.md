## The i-before-e Rule and Its Exceptions

**Rule:** *i* before *e* except after *c*, or when sounding like *a* as in *neighbor* and *weigh*.

| Follows Rule | Exception |
|---|---|
| believe, chief, field, grief, niece, piece, relief, shield, thief | caffeine, either, foreign, height, leisure, neither, protein, seize, species, their, weird |

✅ I before E except after C... and in weird species of foreign leisure.

## Strategy for Improvement

1. **Keep a personal list** — words you consistently misspell.
2. **Learn mnemonics** — *separate has "a rat" in it*.
3. **Read the word backward** — forces you to see each letter.
4. **Use spell-check** — but don't trust it blindly (*their/there/they're* all pass).

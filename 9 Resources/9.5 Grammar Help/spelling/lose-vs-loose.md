# Lose vs Loose

These two words look similar but are pronounced differently and have completely distinct meanings.

## Definitions

- **Lose** (verb, pronounced "looz") — to misplace, to fail to win, to be deprived of.
- **Loose** (adjective, pronounced "loos") — not tight, not firmly attached, free.

## Quick Test

- Can you replace it with *misplace* or *fail to win*? → **lose**
- Can you replace it with *not tight* or *free*? → **loose**

## Examples

### Lose — Verb
✅ I always **lose** my keys. *(misplace)*
✅ The team might **lose** the game. *(fail to win)*
✅ She is trying to **lose** weight. *(get rid of)*
✅ Don't **lose** hope. *(be deprived of)*
✅ He **lost** his job. *(past tense)*
✅ I'm **losing** my mind. *(present participle)*

### Loose — Adjective
✅ This shirt is too **loose**. *(not tight)*
✅ The dog got **loose** from the yard. *(free, unrestrained)*
✅ There's a **loose** wire in the plug. *(not firmly attached)*
✅ She let her hair hang **loose**. *(unrestrained)*

### Loose — Verb (Rare)
*Loose* can also be a verb meaning "to release" or "to set free."
✅ He **loosed** the arrow. *(released — archaic/poetic)*
✅ The prisoner was **loosed** from his chains.

This verb form is rare in modern English; *release*, *set free*, or *let go* are preferred.

## Common Errors

✅ I don't want to **lose** you.
❌ I don't want to **loose** you. *(unless you're untying someone)*

✅ This knot is **loose**.
❌ This knot is **lose**. *(wrong word)*

✅ You're going to **lose** if you don't practice.
❌ You're going to **loose** if you don't practice.

## Memory Tricks

- **Lose** lost an **o** — it only has one. *(When you lose something, one O goes missing.)*
- **Loose** has room to move — two **o**'s are roomier than one.
- Think: *loose* rhymes with *moose*; *lose* rhymes with *choose*.

## Related Pair: Looser vs Loser

- **Looser** (adjective, comparative) — more loose, less tight.
  ✅ This rope is **looser** than the other one.
- **Loser** (noun) — a person who loses.
  ✅ Nobody likes a sore **loser**.
❌ He's such a looser. *(spelling error)*

✅ My belt is too loose — I keep losing my pants.
❌ My belt is too lose — I keep loosing my pants.

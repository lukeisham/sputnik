# Affect vs Effect

These two words are frequently confused. The simplest distinction: *affect* is usually a verb, and *effect* is usually a noun.

## Definitions

- **Affect** (verb) — to influence or produce a change in something.
- **Effect** (noun) — the result or outcome of a change.
- **Effect** (verb, rare) — to bring about or cause to happen (*to effect change*).

## Quick Test
- Can you replace it with *influence*? → Use **affect**.
- Can you replace it with *result* or *outcome*? → Use **effect**.

✅ The weather affects my mood. (influences)
✅ The effect was immediate. (result)

❌ The weather effects my mood.
❌ The affect was immediate.

✅ The new policy will effect meaningful change. (bring about — rare verb form)

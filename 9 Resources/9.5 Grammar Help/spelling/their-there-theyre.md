# Their, There, They're

These three words sound identical but have completely different meanings. Choosing the wrong one is one of the most common writing errors.

## Definitions

- **Their** — possessive form of *they* (belongs to them).
- **There** — a location (*over there*) or existence (*there is/are*).
- **They're** — contraction of *they are*.

## Quick Test
- Can you replace it with *they are*? → Use **they're**.
- Is it about ownership? → Use **their**.
- Is it a place or existence? → Use **there**.

✅ Their house is on the corner. (ownership)
✅ There are three cookies left. (existence)
✅ They're going to the movies. (they are)

❌ There house is on the corner.
❌ Their are three cookies left.
❌ Their going to the movies.

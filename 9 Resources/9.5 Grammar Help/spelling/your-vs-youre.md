# Your vs You're

These two words sound identical but serve different grammatical functions. Mixing them up is a common error that can undermine your credibility.

## Definitions

- **Your** — possessive form of *you* (belongs to you).
- **You're** — contraction of *you are*.

## Quick Test
- Can you replace it with *you are*? → Use **you're**.
- Is it about ownership? → Use **your**.

✅ Your book is on the table. (ownership)
✅ You're going to love this. (you are)

❌ Your going to love this.
❌ You're book is on the table.

✅ Is this your car? (ownership)
✅ I think you're right. (you are)

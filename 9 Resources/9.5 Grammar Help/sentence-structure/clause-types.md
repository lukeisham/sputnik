# Clause Types

A clause is a group of words that **contains both a subject and a verb**. Clauses are the building blocks of sentences.

## Independent Clauses

An independent clause expresses a complete thought and can stand alone as a sentence.

> *She finished her work.*
> *The sun was setting over the hills.*

## Dependent (Subordinate) Clauses

A dependent clause contains a subject and verb but does **not** express a complete thought. It begins with a subordinating conjunction or relative pronoun.

| Type | Begins with | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Adverbial Clause** | *because, although, when, if, while, since, unless, after, before* | *Because it was raining*, we stayed inside. |
| **Adjective (Relative) Clause** | *who, whom, whose, which, that* | The book *that you recommended* was excellent. |
| **Noun Clause** | *that, what, whether, who, why, how* | *What she said* surprised everyone. |

## Restrictive vs Non-restrictive Relative Clauses

- **Restrictive** — Essential to meaning; no commas.
  ✅ The students **who studied** passed the exam. *(only those who studied)*
- **Non-restrictive** — Extra information; set off with commas.
  ✅ The students, **who all studied**, passed the exam. *(all students studied)*

## Clause Parsing Example

> Although she was tired, she finished the report that her boss had requested.

| Clause | Type | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Although she was tired | Adverbial (dependent) | Modifies "finished" |
| she finished the report | Independent | Main clause |
| that her boss had requested | Adjective / Relative (dependent) | Modifies "report" |

✅ When the alarm rang, everyone evacuated the building.
❌ When the alarm rang. Everyone evacuated the building. *(fragment — join)*

# Sentence Types

Sentences are classified by the number and type of clauses they contain.

## Four Sentence Structures

| Type | Clauses | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Simple** | 1 independent | *The cat slept.* |
| **Compound** | 2+ independent | *The cat slept, and the dog watched.* |
| **Complex** | 1 independent + 1+ dependent | *While the cat slept, the dog watched.* |
| **Compound-Complex** | 2+ independent + 1+ dependent | *While the cat slept, the dog watched, and the bird sang.* |

## Compound Sentences — Joining Methods

1. **Coordinating Conjunction** (FANBOYS: *for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so*)
   ✅ I wanted to go, **but** I was too tired.

2. **Semicolon** (closely related clauses)
   ✅ The storm was fierce; the power went out immediately.

3. **Conjunctive Adverb** (semicolon + adverb + comma)
   ✅ The project was difficult; **however**, we finished on time.

## Complex Sentences — Subordinating Patterns

- **Time:** *when, while, after, before, until, since, as soon as*
- **Cause/Effect:** *because, since, so that*
- **Contrast:** *although, even though, whereas, while*
- **Condition:** *if, unless, provided that, in case*

✅ **Although** the deadline was tight, the team delivered.
✅ The team delivered **even though** the deadline was tight.
❌ The deadline was tight, the team delivered. *(comma splice — needs a conjunction or semicolon)*

## Common Errors

| Error | Wrong | Right |
|---|---|---|
| **Run-on Sentence** | It was late I went home. | It was late, so I went home. |
| **Comma Splice** | It was late, I went home. | It was late; I went home. / It was late. I went home. |
| **Fragment** | Because I was tired. | Because I was tired, I went to bed. |

✅ Although it rained, we enjoyed the picnic. *(complex — dependent + independent)*
✅ It rained, but we enjoyed the picnic. *(compound — two independent)*
❌ It rained, we enjoyed the picnic. *(comma splice)*

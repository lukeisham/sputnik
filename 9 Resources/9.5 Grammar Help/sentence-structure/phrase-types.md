# Phrase Types

A phrase is a group of words that functions as a single unit within a sentence. Unlike a clause, a phrase does **not** contain both a subject and a verb.

## Key Phrase Types

1. **Noun Phrase** — A noun and its modifiers (determiners, adjectives, relative clauses).
   - *The tall building on the corner*
   - *Several of the students*

2. **Verb Phrase** — The main verb plus any auxiliaries, objects, or complements.
   - *has been running*
   - *will have finished the report*

3. **Prepositional Phrase** — Begins with a preposition and ends with its object.
   - *under the bridge*
   - *in spite of the weather*

4. **Adjective Phrase** — An adjective and its modifiers.
   - *extremely happy*
   - *proud of her achievement*

5. **Adverb Phrase** — An adverb and its modifiers.
   - *very quickly*
   - *quite unexpectedly*

6. **Gerund Phrase** — A gerund (`-ing` verb acting as a noun) plus objects/modifiers.
   - *Running marathons requires dedication.*
   - *She enjoys reading historical fiction.*

7. **Infinitive Phrase** — An infinitive (`to` + verb) plus objects/modifiers.
   - *To finish on time is our goal.*
   - *He needs to buy groceries.*

8. **Participial Phrase** — A present or past participle plus objects/modifiers.
   - *Walking down the street, she noticed the new café.*
   - *Exhausted from the journey, they went straight to bed.*

## Parsing Example

> The old man in the blue coat was walking very slowly toward the station.

| Segment | Phrase Type |
|---|---|
| The old man | Noun Phrase |
| in the blue coat | Prepositional Phrase |
| was walking | Verb Phrase |
| very slowly | Adverb Phrase |
| toward the station | Prepositional Phrase |

✅ Running every morning improves your health. *(gerund phrase as subject)*
❌ Run every morning improves your health.
✅ She wants to learn Spanish. *(infinitive phrase as object)*
❌ She wants learning Spanish. *(not idiomatic for "wants")*

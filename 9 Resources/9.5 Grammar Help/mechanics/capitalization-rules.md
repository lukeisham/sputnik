# Capitalization Rules

Capitalization signals the start of a sentence and distinguishes proper nouns from common nouns.

## Always Capitalize

1. **First word of a sentence**
   ✅ The meeting starts at noon.

2. **Proper nouns** — specific people, places, organizations, brands
   ✅ Marie Curie, London, United Nations, Apple
   ❌ marie curie, london

3. **Days, months, holidays** (but not seasons)
   ✅ Monday, January, Christmas
   ❌ monday, january
   ✅ summer, autumn, winter, spring *(common nouns — lowercase)*

4. **Titles before names** (but not after or standalone)
   ✅ President Lincoln, Dr. Smith
   ✅ Abraham Lincoln was the president.
   ❌ The President arrived. *(unless referring to a specific head of state in formal context)*

5. **Major words in titles** (title case)
   ✅ *The Catcher in the Rye*
   ✅ *A Tale of Two Cities*

## Do NOT Capitalize

- Common nouns: *city, river, company* (unless part of a proper name)
- Directions: *north, south, east, west* (unless part of a region name — *the South*)
- Academic subjects (except languages): *mathematics, history*
  ✅ I study French and mathematics.
- After a colon (unless what follows is a complete sentence in some style guides)
  ✅ Three things: patience, practice, and persistence.

## Edge Cases

| Scenario | Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Family titles | Capitalize when used as a name | ✅ I asked Mother. / ❌ I asked my mother. |
| Internet/Web | Style-guide dependent | ✅ internet / Internet *(both accepted)* |
| Racial/Ethnic groups | Capitalize | ✅ Asian, Black, Indigenous, Latino |
| Historical periods | Capitalize named periods | ✅ the Renaissance, the Middle Ages |
| Brands with stylized casing | Preserve or normalize | ✅ iPhone, eBay *(preserve)* / Iphone, Ebay *(normalize in all-caps contexts)* |

✅ She visited the Eiffel Tower in Paris last summer.
❌ She visited the eiffel tower in paris last Summer.
✅ I asked Dad if I could borrow the car.
❌ I asked my Dad if I could borrow the car. *(used as relationship, not name)*

# Writing Numbers

Style guides differ on when to spell out numbers and when to use numerals. These are the most widely accepted conventions.

## General Rules (AP/Chicago Hybrid)

| Range | Rule | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Zero to nine | Spell out | ✅ three apples, seven days |
| 10 and above | Use numerals | ✅ 15 people, 342 pages |
| At start of sentence | Always spell out | ✅ Forty-two students attended. |
| Mixed in same sentence | Be consistent | ✅ We need 5 chairs and 12 tables. |

## Special Cases

1. **Percentages** — Use numerals + *%* (AP style)
   ✅ 5%, 3.5 percent *(spell out "percent" in formal writing)*

2. **Money**
   ✅ $5, $3.50, 50 cents *(consistent within a passage)*

3. **Dates and Times**
   ✅ June 8, 2026 / 8 June 2026
   ✅ 4 p.m. / 4:00 PM *(consistent style)*
   ✅ the 1990s / the '90s *(not 1990's)*

4. **Ages**
   ✅ She is 7 years old. / a seven-year-old child
   ✅ He is in his 30s.

5. **Fractions**
   ✅ one-half, two-thirds *(spell out simple fractions)*
   ✅ 3½ cups *(use numerals with symbols)*

6. **Measurements**
   ✅ 5 feet 3 inches / 5'3"
   ✅ 15 kilometers, 3.5 kg

7. **Large Round Numbers**
   ✅ 3 million, 12 billion *(not 3,000,000 unless precision matters)*

## Common Errors

✅ The recipe calls for 2 cups of flour and one teaspoon of salt.
❌ The recipe calls for two cups of flour and 1 teaspoon of salt. *(inconsistent)*

✅ Only 3 percent of respondents disagreed.
❌ Only three % of respondents disagreed.

✅ The 1980s were a decade of change.
❌ The 1980's were a decade of change. *(no apostrophe for plural decades)*

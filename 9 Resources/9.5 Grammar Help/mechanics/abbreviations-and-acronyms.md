# Abbreviations and Acronyms

Abbreviations shorten words; acronyms form pronounceable words from initials. Both follow specific formatting conventions.

## Types

| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Acronym** | Pronounced as a word | NASA, NATO, SCUBA |
| **Initialism** | Pronounced letter-by-letter | FBI, CEO, HTML, BBC |
| **Contraction** | Middle letters omitted | Dr., Mr., St. (street) |
| **Shortening** | End truncated | prof., approx., etc. |

## When to Use Abbreviations

### Acceptable
✅ Well-known abbreviations in formal writing: *PhD, CEO, a.m./p.m., AD/BC*
✅ Technical terms after first full mention: *Content Management System (CMS)... the CMS...*
✅ Latin abbreviations in notes and parenthetical: *e.g., i.e., etc., et al.*
✅ Units of measurement with numerals: *15 km, 100 ml*

### Avoid
❌ Informal abbreviations in formal writing: *wanna, gonna, kinda*
❌ Over-abbreviating: *The mgr. of the dept. approved.*
❌ Unfamiliar abbreviations without definition

## Period Usage

| Rule | Example |
|---|---|
| **US English:** periods in most abbreviations | Mr., Mrs., Dr., a.m., i.e., e.g. |
| **UK English:** omit periods if first and last letter match | Mr, Mrs, Dr, ie, eg |
| **Acronyms and initialisms:** no periods | NASA, FBI, UN *(not N.A.S.A.)* |

## Common Pitfalls

✅ The CEO of IBM gave a speech. *(no periods in acronyms)*
❌ The C.E.O. of I.B.M. gave a speech.

✅ She has a PhD in physics.
❌ She has a Ph.D. in physics. *(style-dependent; check your guide)*

✅ Please bring paper, pens, pencils, etc.
❌ Please bring paper, pens, pencils, and etc. *(et = "and" — redundant)*

✅ We need several items (e.g., paper, pens, and folders).
✅ We need several items (i.e., only office supplies).
❌ We need several items (i.e. paper, pens). *(missing comma in US style)*

## Edge Cases

| Abbreviation | Full Form | Note |
|---|---|---|
| e.g. | *exempli gratia* | "for example" |
| i.e. | *id est* | "that is" / "in other words" |
| etc. | *et cetera* | "and other things" |
| et al. | *et alia* | "and others" (people) |
| sic | *sic erat scriptum* | "thus was it written" — indicates a quoted error is original |
| viz. | *videlicet* | "namely" / "to wit" |

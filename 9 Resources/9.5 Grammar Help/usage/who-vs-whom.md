# Who vs Whom

*Who* is a subject pronoun (like *he, she, they*); *whom* is an object pronoun (like *him, her, them*). The distinction is formal — in casual speech, *who* is often used for both.

## Key Rules

1. **Subject = Who** — Use *who* when the pronoun is doing the action.
2. **Object = Whom** — Use *whom* when the pronoun is receiving the action or follows a preposition.
3. **The Him Test** — Rephrase the sentence; if *him* sounds right, use *whom*; if *he* sounds right, use *who*.

✅ Who wrote the letter? (he wrote → who)
✅ To whom should I address this? (to him → whom)

❌ Whom wrote the letter?
❌ To who should I address this?

✅ The person who called earlier left a message.
✅ The person whom you called is not available. (*you called him* → whom)

# Then vs Than

These words look and sound similar but serve completely different grammatical functions.

## Definitions

- **Then** — relates to **time** or **sequence** (what happens next).
- **Than** — used for **comparisons** (comparing two things).

## Quick Test

- Is there a comparison happening? *(bigger, better, more, less, rather)* → **than**
- Is it about time or sequence? *(next, afterward, at that time)* → **then**

## Examples

### Then — Time / Sequence
✅ We went to dinner, **then** we saw a movie. *(sequence)*
✅ Finish your homework, **then** you can play. *(next)*
✅ Back **then**, things were different. *(at that time)*
✅ If it rains, **then** we'll stay inside. *(consequence)*
✅ The **then**-president signed the bill. *(at that time — adjective)*

### Then — Additional Meanings
✅ The cost is $100, **then** there's tax. *(in addition)*
✅ Well **then**, let's begin. *(in that case)*

### Than — Comparison
✅ She is taller **than** her brother.
✅ I'd rather walk **than** drive.
✅ It's easier said **than** done.
✅ More **than** 100 people attended.
✅ No sooner had I arrived **than** it started raining.

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| He is bigger then me. | He is bigger **than** me. |
| I'd rather go then stay. | I'd rather go **than** stay. |
| First we eat, than we'll talk. | First we eat, **then** we'll talk. |
| More then ever, we need change. | More **than** ever, we need change. |

## Tricky Edge Cases

### "Other than" / "rather than" / "more than"
Always use **than**:
✅ Other **than** that, everything is fine.
✅ I'd prefer coffee rather **than** tea.
✅ It's more **than** enough.

### "Now and then" / "Every now and then"
Always use **then**:
✅ I think about it now and **then**.
✅ Every now and **then**, she calls.

### "No sooner... than"
The fixed idiom is *no sooner... than* (not *then*):
✅ No sooner had I sat down **than** the phone rang.
❌ No sooner had I sat down **then** the phone rang.

### "Then" as a conditional
✅ If you study, **then** you'll pass. *(consequence, not comparison)*
✅ If you're unhappy, **then** leave. *(in that case)*

## Memory Tricks

- **ThEn** relates to timE.
- **ThAn** is used for compArisons.

✅ We ate dinner; **then** we had dessert. *(sequence)*
✅ Dessert was better **than** dinner. *(comparison)*
❌ Dessert was better then dinner. *(wrong — this is a comparison)*

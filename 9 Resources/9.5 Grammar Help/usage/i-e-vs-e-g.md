# i.e. vs e.g.

These two Latin abbreviations are frequently swapped. They mean completely different things.

## Definitions

| Abbreviation | Latin | English | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **e.g.** | *exempli gratia* | "for example" | Give one or more examples |
| **i.e.** | *id est* | "that is" / "in other words" | Clarify or restate what you just said |

## Quick Test

- Can you replace it with *"for example"*? → **e.g.**
- Can you replace it with *"in other words"* or *"that is"*? → **i.e.**

## Examples

### e.g. — For Example
✅ I love citrus fruits (e.g., oranges, lemons, and grapefruits).
✅ Bring something to share — e.g., snacks or drinks.

### i.e. — That Is
✅ My favorite citrus fruit (i.e., the one I eat every day) is the orange.
✅ The project is due ASAP — i.e., by Friday at noon.

## Contrast

✅ She studies Romance languages (e.g., French, Spanish, Italian). *(examples — there are more)*
✅ She studies Romance languages (i.e., languages derived from Latin). *(explanation/definition)*

✅ We need to reduce costs — i.e., cut the marketing budget by 20%. *(specific clarification)*
✅ We need to reduce costs — e.g., by cutting the marketing budget or renegotiating contracts. *(one possible example)*

## Punctuation

| Style | e.g. | i.e. |
|---|---|---|
| **US** | e.g., *(with comma after)* | i.e., *(with comma after)* |
| **UK** | eg, *(no periods, comma)* | ie, *(no periods, comma)* |

Always follow with a comma in US English.

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| I like outdoor sports (i.e., hiking, biking, swimming). | I like outdoor sports (**e.g.**, hiking, biking, swimming). | You're listing examples, not restating. |
| My favorite outdoor sport (e.g., hiking) keeps me fit. | My favorite outdoor sport (**i.e.**, hiking) keeps me fit. | You're specifying, not giving an example. |
| Bring items i.e. a tent, flashlight, and water. | Bring items **e.g.**, a tent, flashlight, and water. | Examples of items. |
| We need to improve our ROI, e.g., we need to make more money. | We need to improve our ROI, **i.e.**, we need to make more money. | Restating, not exemplifying. |

## Style Advice

In running text, many style guides recommend using the English equivalents instead of the Latin abbreviations:

- Instead of *e.g.*, write *for example* or *such as*.
- Instead of *i.e.*, write *that is* or *in other words*.

This is especially true for writing aimed at a general audience who may not know the difference.

✅ Several factors (e.g., budget, timeline, and staffing) affect the decision.
✅ Several factors (such as budget, timeline, and staffing) affect the decision.

✅ The key metric — i.e., customer satisfaction — improved.
✅ The key metric — that is, customer satisfaction — improved.

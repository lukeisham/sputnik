# Singular They

The use of *they/them/their* as a singular pronoun has a long history in English. It is now widely accepted as both a gender-neutral option when gender is unknown and as the pronoun for individuals who use *they* as their personal pronoun.

## Three Uses

### 1. Generic/Unknown Gender (Historical)
Used when the gender of the antecedent is unknown or irrelevant. This usage dates back to the 14th century (Chaucer, Shakespeare, Austen all used it).

✅ Someone left **their** umbrella. Can you return it to **them**?
✅ Every student should bring **their** own laptop.
✅ If a customer calls, tell **them** I'll be back soon.

### 2. Personal Pronoun (Modern)
Used by individuals whose gender identity is non-binary or who prefer *they* as their pronoun.

✅ Alex said **they** would arrive at noon. *(Alex uses they/them pronouns)*
✅ I spoke to Jordan, and **they** approved the budget. **Their** decision is final.

### 3. Generic Alternatives (Formal/Traditional)
Before *singular they* was widely accepted, writers used alternatives — each with drawbacks:

| Method | Example | Issue |
|---|---|---|
| *he or she* | Each student must bring his or her book. | Clumsy when repeated |
| *he* (generic) | Each student must bring his book. | Excludes women |
| *she* (alternating) | The doctor should listen to her patient. | Still gendered |
| *one* | One should do one's best. | Stilted |
| Rewrite as plural | All students must bring their books. | Often the best fix |

## Verb Agreement

Even with a singular antecedent, *they* takes plural verb forms:

✅ Someone is at the door — **they are** waiting.
✅ If a person wants to succeed, **they need** to work hard.
❌ Someone is at the door — they is waiting. *(always "they are")*

## Reflexive Form

✅ Someone left a note but didn't sign **themself**.
✅ Someone left a note but didn't sign **themselves**.

Both *themself* and *themselves* are used. *Themself* is gaining ground for singular they; *themselves* is the traditional form for both singular and plural they.

## Style Guide Acceptance

| Style Guide | Stance |
|---|---|
| **APA (7th ed.)** | Endorses singular they |
| **Chicago (17th ed.)** | Accepts as last resort; prefers rewording |
| **AP Stylebook (2017)** | Endorses singular they |
| **MLA (8th ed.)** | Endorses singular they |
| **Oxford** | Accepts with caution |
| **Merriam-Webster** | Endorses; named Word of the Year 2019 |

✅ Every employee must complete **their** training by Friday.
❌ Every employee must complete his training by Friday. *(exclusionary)*
✅ If anyone disagrees, **they** should speak up.
❌ If anyone disagrees, he should speak up. *(assumes gender)*

# Irregular Verbs

Most English verbs are regular — they form the past tense and past participle by adding *-ed* or *-d*. Irregular verbs do not follow this pattern and must be memorized.

## Common Patterns

### Pattern 1: No Change
| Base | Past | Past Participle |
|---|---|---|
| cut | cut | cut |
| put | put | put |
| set | set | set |
| shut | shut | shut |
| read | read (pron. *red*) | read (pron. *red*) |

### Pattern 2: One Vowel Change
| Base | Past | Past Participle |
|---|---|---|
| sing | sang | sung |
| drink | drank | drunk |
| begin | began | begun |
| swim | swam | swum |
| ring | rang | rung |

### Pattern 3: *-ought* / *-aught*
| Base | Past | Past Participle |
|---|---|---|
| buy | bought | bought |
| bring | brought | brought |
| think | thought | thought |
| catch | caught | caught |
| teach | taught | taught |

### Pattern 4: *-en* Past Participle
| Base | Past | Past Participle |
|---|---|---|
| break | broke | broken |
| speak | spoke | spoken |
| steal | stole | stolen |
| write | wrote | written |
| drive | drove | driven |

### Pattern 5: Two Different Forms
| Base | Past | Past Participle |
|---|---|---|
| go | went | gone |
| be | was/were | been |
| do | did | done |
| see | saw | seen |
| lie (recline) | lay | lain |

## Most Commonly Confused Irregulars

✅ I **lay** down an hour ago. *(past of "lie" — to recline)*
✅ I have **lain** here all day.
❌ I **laid** down an hour ago. *(laid = past of "lay" — to place something)*

✅ The ship **sank** in the storm.
✅ The ship has **sunk**.
❌ The ship **sunk** yesterday. *(need simple past "sank")*

✅ I **saw** the movie.
✅ I have **seen** it three times.
❌ I **seen** the movie. *(missing auxiliary)*

✅ He **hung** the picture. *(hang — to suspend)*
✅ They **hanged** the criminal. *(hang — to execute)*
❌ They **hung** the criminal. *(execution uses "hanged")*

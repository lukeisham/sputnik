# Collective Nouns

Collective nouns refer to a group as a single entity (*team, family, committee, audience*). Whether they take a singular or plural verb depends on whether the group is acting as a unit or as individuals.

## The Core Rule

| Use | Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Group acting **as one unit** | Singular | The team **is** winning. |
| Members acting **individually** | Plural | The team **are** arguing among themselves. |

## US vs UK Convention

- **US English:** Strongly prefers singular.
  ✅ The committee **has** reached a decision.
  ✅ The band **is** touring next month.

- **UK English:** Often uses plural with collective nouns.
  ✅ The committee **have** reached a decision.
  ✅ The band **are** touring next month.

Both are grammatically defensible — choose one convention and be consistent.

## Common Collective Nouns

| Noun | Example (Singular) | Example (Plural) |
|---|---|---|
| team | The team **plays** well. | The team **disagree** on strategy. |
| family | My family **lives** in London. | My family **are** scattered across the country. |
| committee | The committee **meets** on Tuesday. | The committee **have** differing opinions. |
| audience | The audience **was** captivated. | The audience **were** clapping at different times. |
| staff | The staff **is** dedicated. | The staff **are** taking their breaks. |
| government | The government **has** decided. | The government **are** divided on the issue. |
| jury | The jury **has** reached a verdict. | The jury **are** still deliberating. |
| class | The class **is** studying grammar. | The class **are** working on individual projects. |

## Number Agreement Within a Sentence

Once you choose singular or plural, be consistent:

✅ The committee **has** made **its** decision. *(singular throughout)*
✅ The committee **have** made **their** decision. *(plural throughout)*
❌ The committee **has** made **their** decision. *(mixed — avoid)*

## Tricky Cases

### Names of Organizations and Countries
✅ The United Nations **is** headquartered in New York. *(treated as singular)*
✅ The Netherlands **is** a small country. *(singular, despite plural form)*

### *Police* — Always Plural
✅ The police **are** investigating. *(never "The police is...")*
✅ The police **have** arrived.

### *Number* — Singular vs Plural
✅ A number of issues **are** unresolved. *(a number = several → plural)*
✅ The number of issues **is** growing. *(the number = the quantity → singular)*

### *Couple* — Context Dependent
✅ The couple **is** getting married. *(as a unit)*
✅ The couple **were** arguing all night. *(as individuals)*

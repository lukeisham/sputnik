# Subjunctive Mood

The subjunctive mood expresses wishes, hypotheticals, demands, and suggestions — situations that are contrary to fact or not yet real. English uses it far less than languages like Spanish or French, but it still matters in formal and academic writing.

## Present Subjunctive

Used after verbs and expressions of **demand, suggestion, requirement, or necessity**.

**Form:** The base form of the verb (no *-s* in the third person).

✅ I suggest that he **arrive** on time. *(not "arrives")*
✅ The committee demands that she **be** present.
✅ It is essential that every student **attend**.
✅ We recommend that the report **be** submitted by Friday.

❌ I suggest that he arrives on time. *(indicative — sounds wrong in formal contexts)*
❌ She demanded that he is there. *(should be "be")*

### Common Triggers
Verbs: *ask, command, demand, insist, propose, recommend, request, suggest*
Adjectives: *essential, important, imperative, necessary, vital*

## Past Subjunctive

Used for **wishes** and **hypotheticals** — things that are not true or unlikely.

**Form:** Identical to simple past, except *were* is used for all persons.

✅ I wish I **were** taller.
✅ If I **were** you, I would accept the offer.
✅ She talks as though she **were** the boss.

❌ I wish I was taller. *(informal; subjunctive "were" is formally correct)*
❌ If I was you... *(always wrong in this fixed phrase)*

## The Fixed Phrase Exception

After *if*, *as if*, and *as though*, use *were* (not *was*) for hypotheticals:

✅ If it **were** possible, we would help.
✅ He behaves as if he **owned** the place.
✅ Suppose she **were** to arrive early.

## Subjunctive in *That* Clauses

In formal writing, after expressions of emotion, judgment, or necessity:

✅ It is surprising that he **be** so calm.
✅ It is a shame that she **leave** so soon. *(formal/literary)*

Most modern speakers use *should* + infinitive or the indicative instead:
✅ It is surprising that he **should be** so calm.
✅ It is surprising that he **is** so calm.

## Common Phrases in the Subjunctive

| Phrase | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *Be that as it may...* | Regardless |
| *Come what may...* | Whatever happens |
| *Heaven forbid...* | I hope not |
| *Suffice it to say...* | In short |
| *God save the King.* | Long live the King |
| *Long live...* | May ... live long |
| *So be it.* | Let it be so |

✅ The doctor recommended that she **take** more rest.
✅ If I **were** in charge, I would change the policy.
❌ If I **was** in charge, I would change the policy. *(subjunctive preferred in formal writing)*

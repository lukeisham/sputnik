# Split Infinitives

A split infinitive occurs when a word — usually an adverb — is placed between *to* and the verb. The classic example: *to boldly go*.

## The "Rule" and Its Origin

The prohibition against split infinitives is a **zombie rule** — one that survives despite having no basis in English grammar. It originated in the 19th century when grammarians tried to force English to follow Latin rules. In Latin, infinitives are a single word (*amāre* = "to love") and cannot be split. English is not Latin.

## When Splitting Is Correct

### 1. Splitting avoids ambiguity
✅ She decided **to gradually reduce** her workload. *(clear)*
❌ She decided gradually to reduce her workload. *(awkward, ambiguous — gradually decided?)*
❌ She decided to reduce gradually her workload. *(awkward)*

### 2. Splitting sounds natural
✅ I want **to really understand** this concept.
❌ I want really to understand this concept.
❌ I want to understand really this concept.

### 3. Splitting preserves emphasis
✅ We need **to more than double** our output.
❌ We need to double more than our output. *(nonsensical)*

### 4. Splitting is idiomatic
✅ He expects **to at least break even**.
❌ He expects at least to break even. *(changes meaning — expectation is minimum, not the breaking even)*

## When to Avoid Splitting

### 1. Lazy splitting with long adverbial phrases
❌ She wanted **to, despite all the warnings from her colleagues, proceed**.
✅ Despite all the warnings from her colleagues, she wanted to proceed.

### 2. In very formal or traditional contexts (know your audience)
Some readers still cringe. In highly conservative writing (legal documents, traditional academic style), you can unsplit to avoid distracting those readers:

- Split: *to fully investigate*
- Unsplit: *to investigate fully* or *fully to investigate*

## Famous Split Infinitives

| Quote | Source |
|---|---|
| "**To boldly go** where no man has gone before." | Star Trek |
| "I want **to really emphasize** this point." | Common |
| "The company expects **to more than triple** its revenue." | Business writing |

## Verdict

The split infinitive "rule" is a myth. Write what sounds natural and clear. If splitting makes the sentence better — split. If a reader will be distracted or the split is egregiously long — unsplit.

✅ We need **to carefully review** the data before publishing.
✅ We need to review the data carefully before publishing. *(both fine)*
✅ The goal is **to completely eliminate** waste by next year.
❌ The goal is completely to eliminate waste by next year. *(unnecessarily stilted)*

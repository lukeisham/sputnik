# Interjections

Interjections are short exclamatory words or phrases that express emotion, reaction, or emphasis. They are grammatically independent — they stand apart from the sentence structure.

## Strong vs Mild Interjections

| Strength | Examples | Emotion |
|---|---|---|
| **Strong** | *Wow! Ouch! Oh no! Darn! Yikes!* | Followed by an exclamation mark |
| **Mild** | *Oh, Hmm, Well, Ah, Gosh* | Followed by a comma |

Strong interjections can stand alone as a sentence; mild ones typically attach to the sentence that follows.

✅ **Wow!** That's amazing.
✅ **Oh,** I didn't see you there.
✅ **Ouch!** That hurt.
✅ **Hmm,** I'm not sure about that.

## Common Interjections by Emotion

### Joy / Excitement
✅ **Wow!** You won the prize!
✅ **Yay!** We're going to the beach!
✅ **Woo-hoo!** I passed the exam!

### Surprise / Shock
✅ **Oh!** You scared me.
✅ **Gosh,** I didn't expect that.
✅ **What!** Are you serious?
✅ **Really?** I can't believe it.

### Pain / Displeasure
✅ **Ouch!** That's hot!
✅ **Ow,** that hurts.
✅ **Ugh,** I have to work late again.
✅ **Yuck!** This tastes terrible.

### Understanding / Realization
✅ **Ah,** now I understand.
✅ **Aha!** So that's the trick.
✅ **Oh,** I see what you mean.

### Hesitation / Thinking
✅ **Well,** let me think about it.
✅ **Hmm,** that's a good question.
✅ **Um,** I'm not sure.
✅ **Er,** what was I saying?

### Dismissal / Indifference
✅ **Meh,** it's not that great.
✅ **Eh,** it could be worse.
✅ **Whatever,** I don't care.

### Agreement / Acknowledgment
✅ **Okay,** I'll do it.
✅ **Yes!** That's exactly right.
✅ **Right,** let's get started.
✅ **Uh-huh,** I'm listening.

### Disgust / Disapproval
✅ **Eww!** That's gross.
✅ **Blech,** this coffee is cold.
✅ **Phew,** that smell is awful.

## Punctuation of Interjections

### Strong Emotion — Exclamation Mark
✅ **Yikes!** That was close.
✅ **Brilliant!** What a performance.

### Mild Emotion — Comma
✅ **Oh,** I didn't know that.
✅ **Well,** it's worth a try.

### Mid-Sentence — Dashes or Parentheses
✅ The speech — **phew** — lasted three hours.
✅ His answer (**ahem**) was not entirely honest.

## Interjections in Dialogue

Interjections are common in speech and dialogue but should be used **sparingly** in formal writing:

❌ The quarterly report — wow — exceeded expectations. *(too informal)*
✅ The quarterly report far exceeded expectations.

✅ "**Well,** you see, it's not that simple," she said. *(natural in dialogue)*
✅ "**Ouch!** Watch where you're going!" he yelled. *(natural in dialogue)*

## When to Avoid Interjections

- Academic writing (papers, essays, theses)
- Professional reports and business correspondence
- Technical documentation and manuals
- Formal announcements

✅ The results were unexpected but significant. *(no interjection needed)*
❌ Wow! The results were unexpected but significant.

## Interjections vs Onomatopoeia

Interjections express emotion; onomatopoeia imitates sounds.

| Interjection (emotion) | Onomatopoeia (sound) |
|---|---|
| **Ouch!** (pain) | **Bang!** (loud noise) |
| **Yikes!** (fear) | **Buzz** (insect/device) |
| **Phew!** (relief) | **Whoosh** (air/wind) |
| **Ahem** (attention) | **Cough** (throat sound) |

Some words can function as both depending on context:

✅ **Pop!** The balloon burst. *(onomatopoeia)*
✅ **Pop!** I almost had a heart attack. *(interjection — surprise)*

# Adjectives

Adjectives describe or modify nouns and pronouns. They answer the questions *Which one?*, *What kind?*, and *How many?* Adjectives can appear before a noun (attributive) or after a linking verb (predicative).

## Key Rules

1. **Attributive Placement** — Place adjectives directly before the noun they modify.
2. **Predicative Placement** — After linking verbs (*be, seem, become, feel*), the adjective describes the subject.
3. **Coordinate Adjectives** — When two adjectives equally modify a noun, separate them with a comma (or *and*).
4. **Cumulative Adjectives** — When adjectives build on each other (size → age → color), do not use a comma.
5. **Comparative & Superlative** — Use *-er / -est* for short adjectives; use *more / most* for longer ones.

## Positive, Comparative, Superlative

| Positive | Comparative | Superlative |
|---|---|---|
| tall | taller | tallest |
| happy | happier | happiest |
| beautiful | more beautiful | most beautiful |
| good | better | best |
| bad | worse | worst |
| far | farther/further | farthest/furthest |

## Coordinate vs Cumulative Adjectives

✅ It was a **cold, rainy** day. (coordinate — comma needed)
✅ She wore a **beautiful red** dress. (cumulative — no comma)

**Quick test**: Can you swap the adjectives? If the sentence still reads naturally, they are coordinate and need a comma.

✅ It was a rainy, cold day. (swap works → coordinate, comma)
❌ She wore a red beautiful dress. (swap fails → cumulative, no comma)

## Common Mistakes

### Adjective vs Adverb After Linking Verbs
✅ I **feel bad** about the mistake. (adjective modifying *I*)
❌ I feel **badly** about the mistake. (adverb — implies poor sense of touch)

### Double Comparatives
✅ She is **more talented** than her brother.
❌ She is **more talenteder** than her brother.

### Absolute Adjectives
Some adjectives cannot be logically compared:
✅ That design is **unique**.
❌ That design is **more unique**.
✅ That design is **more unusual**.

Other absolute adjectives: *perfect, complete, impossible, eternal, pregnant*.

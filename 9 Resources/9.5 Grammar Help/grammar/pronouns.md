# Pronouns

Pronouns replace nouns and must agree with their antecedents in number, gender, and case. Case determines whether a pronoun is a subject (*I, he, she, we, they*) or an object (*me, him, her, us, them*).

## Key Rules

1. **Subject vs Object** — Use subject pronouns before verbs; use object pronouns after verbs and prepositions.
2. **I vs Me** — After a preposition or as the object of a verb, use *me* not *I*.
3. **Who vs Whom** — Use *who* for subjects, *whom* for objects (see the Who vs Whom topic for details).
4. **Pronoun Agreement** — A pronoun must match its antecedent in number (*everyone → their* is now accepted in modern usage).

✅ She and I went to the park.
❌ Me and her went to the park.

✅ The gift is for you and me.
❌ The gift is for you and I.

✅ They asked him and me to help.
❌ They asked him and I to help.

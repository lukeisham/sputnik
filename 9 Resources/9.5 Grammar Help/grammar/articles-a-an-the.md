# Articles: A, An, The

Articles are the most common determiners in English. They signal whether a noun is specific (*the*) or general (*a/an*).

## Indefinite Articles: *A* vs *An*

Use *a* before consonant **sounds** and *an* before vowel **sounds** — based on pronunciation, not spelling.

✅ **a** university *(starts with "yoo" sound)*
✅ **an** umbrella *(starts with "uh" sound)*
✅ **a** European *(starts with "yoo" sound)*
✅ **an** hour *(silent "h" — starts with "ow" sound)*
✅ **a** one-time offer *(starts with "w" sound)*
❌ an university
❌ a hour

### Edge Cases

| Word | Article | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| herb | **an** herb (US) / **a** herb (UK) | US drops the "h" |
| historic | **a** historic (common) / **an** historic (formal/old-fashioned) | Depends on whether "h" is pronounced |
| FBI | **an** FBI agent | "eff" is a vowel sound |
| UFO | **a** UFO | "yoo" is a consonant sound |
| MBA | **an** MBA | "em" is a vowel sound |

## Definite Article: *The*

Use *the* when the listener/reader knows which specific thing you're referring to.

✅ **The** sun rises in the east. *(only one)*
✅ I saw a dog. **The** dog was brown. *(second mention — now specific)*
✅ **The** book you recommended was excellent. *(specified by the relative clause)*
✅ **The** President gave a speech. *(unique role)*

## No Article (Zero Article)

Omit articles with:

| Use | Example |
|---|---|
| General plural nouns | ✅ Dogs are loyal. *(not "The dogs")* |
| Uncountable nouns (general) | ✅ Water is essential. |
| Proper nouns (most) | ✅ London, Microsoft, Mount Everest |
| Meals (general) | ✅ Let's have lunch. *(but: The lunch was delicious.)* |
| Languages and subjects | ✅ She speaks French. |
| Sports and games | ✅ I play tennis. |

## Common Errors

| Error | Correction | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| The life is beautiful. | Life is beautiful. | Abstract nouns take no article |
| She is the teacher. | She is a teacher. | First mention of a profession |
| I went to a school. | I went to school. / I went to the school. | "to school" = attend as student; "to the school" = visit the building |
| He is in a hospital. | He is in hospital. (UK) / He is in the hospital. (US) | UK: "in hospital" = as a patient; US: always "in the hospital" |

✅ I need **an** honest answer.
✅ He is **a** European diplomat.
❌ He is **an** European diplomat.

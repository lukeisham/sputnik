# Conjunctions

Conjunctions connect words, phrases, clauses, or sentences. There are three main types: coordinating, subordinating, and correlative conjunctions.

## Coordinating Conjunctions

The seven coordinating conjunctions can be remembered with **FANBOYS**: **F**or, **A**nd, **N**or, **B**ut, **O**r, **Y**et, **S**o. They join equal grammatical elements.

✅ I wanted to go, **but** I was too tired.
✅ She likes coffee **and** tea.
✅ You can have cake **or** ice cream.
❌ I wanted to go, **but** was too tired. *(missing subject in second clause)*

### Comma Rules with Coordinating Conjunctions

Use a comma before a coordinating conjunction when it joins two independent clauses:

✅ The sun set, **and** the stars appeared.
❌ The sun set **and** the stars appeared. *(comma needed)*

Do **not** use a comma when joining two words or short phrases:

✅ She bought apples **and** oranges.
❌ She bought apples, **and** oranges.

## Subordinating Conjunctions

Subordinating conjunctions (*because, although, while, since, if, when, unless, etc.*) connect a dependent clause to an independent clause.

### When the dependent clause comes first, use a comma:

✅ **Because** it was raining, we stayed inside.
✅ **Although** she was tired, she finished the project.

### When the independent clause comes first, no comma is needed:

✅ We stayed inside **because** it was raining.
✅ She finished the project **although** she was tired.

### Common Subordinating Conjunctions

| Conjunction | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| because | reason | ✅ We left **because** it was late. |
| although / though | contrast | ✅ **Although** it was cold, we went outside. |
| while | simultaneous action | ✅ She read **while** he cooked. |
| since | time or reason | ✅ **Since** you're here, let's start. |
| if | condition | ✅ **If** it rains, the game will be cancelled. |
| unless | exception | ✅ **Unless** you object, we'll proceed. |
| when | time | ✅ Call me **when** you arrive. |
| whereas | contrast (formal) | ✅ She prefers tea, **whereas** he drinks coffee. |

## Correlative Conjunctions

These work in pairs: *both…and, either…or, neither…nor, not only…but also, whether…or*.

### Keep the structure parallel after each part:

✅ She is **both** intelligent **and** hardworking.
❌ She is **both** intelligent **and** she works hard. *(not parallel)*

✅ You can **either** pay now **or** pay later.
✅ He **not only** won the race **but also** broke the record.
❌ He **not only** won the race **but also** he broke the record. *(not parallel)*

### Subject-Verb Agreement with Correlative Conjunctions

With *either…or* and *neither…nor*, the verb agrees with the subject closest to it:

✅ **Neither** the manager **nor** the employees **were** satisfied.
✅ **Neither** the employees **nor** the manager **was** satisfied.

## Common Mistakes

### Comma Splice (Two Independent Clauses Joined Only by a Comma)
❌ The meeting ended, everyone left.
✅ The meeting ended, **so** everyone left.
✅ The meeting ended; everyone left.

### Starting a Sentence with a Conjunction
This is **acceptable** in modern writing — but use it sparingly for emphasis:

✅ **And** that's why we chose this path.
✅ **But** there was one problem.
❌ **(Overusing it makes writing feel choppy.)**

### Because vs Because Of
✅ We cancelled **because** it was raining. *(clause)*
✅ We cancelled **because of** the rain. *(noun phrase)*
❌ We cancelled **because** the rain.

### Than vs Then
✅ She is taller **than** I am. *(comparison)*
✅ First we ate, **then** we left. *(time)*
❌ She is taller **then** I am.

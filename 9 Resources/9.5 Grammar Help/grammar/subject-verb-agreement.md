# Subject-Verb Agreement

The subject and verb in a sentence must agree in number: singular subjects take singular verbs, and plural subjects take plural verbs.

## Key Rules

1. **Singular Subjects** — Add *-s* or *-es* to the verb (e.g., *he runs, she watches*).
2. **Plural Subjects** — Do not add *-s* to the verb (e.g., *they run, we watch*).
3. **Compound Subjects** — Two subjects joined by *and* take a plural verb; subjects joined by *or/nor* agree with the nearest subject.
4. **Indefinite Pronouns** — *Everyone, somebody, each, neither* take singular verbs.
5. **Intervening Phrases** — Ignore phrases between the subject and verb (e.g., *The box of chocolates is...*).

✅ The list of items is on the desk.
❌ The list of items are on the desk.

✅ Everyone in the room knows the answer.
❌ Everyone in the room know the answer.

✅ Neither the teacher nor the students were late.
✅ Neither the students nor the teacher was late.

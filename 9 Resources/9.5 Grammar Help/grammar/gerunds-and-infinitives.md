# Gerunds and Infinitives

Gerunds (*-ing* forms acting as nouns) and infinitives (*to* + base verb) can both function as subjects, objects, and complements. Some verbs take only gerunds, some only infinitives, and some can take either — sometimes with a change in meaning.

## Gerund (-ing)

A gerund is a verb form ending in *-ing* that functions as a **noun**.

✅ **Running** is good exercise. *(subject)*
✅ She enjoys **reading**. *(direct object)*
✅ His hobby is **painting**. *(subject complement)*
✅ She is good **at dancing**. *(object of a preposition)*

## Infinitive (to + verb)

An infinitive can function as a noun, adjective, or adverb.

✅ **To travel** is her dream. *(subject — usually sounds more formal)*
✅ She wants **to leave**. *(direct object)*
✅ He has a report **to write**. *(adjective — modifies "report")*
✅ She studied hard **to pass**. *(adverb — expresses purpose)*

## Verbs Followed Only by Gerunds

| Verb | Example |
|---|---|
| admit | He admitted **stealing** the money. |
| avoid | She avoids **eating** sugar. |
| consider | We considered **moving** to a larger office. |
| deny | He denied **knowing** anything. |
| enjoy | I enjoy **hiking** on weekends. |
| finish | She finished **writing** the book. |
| mind | Do you mind **waiting**? |
| miss | I miss **living** near the beach. |
| postpone | They postponed **making** a decision. |
| practice | You should practice **speaking** French. |
| recommend | I recommend **arriving** early. |
| risk | Don't risk **losing** everything. |
| suggest | She suggested **taking** a break. |

❌ She avoids to eat sugar.
❌ I enjoy to hike on weekends.

## Verbs Followed Only by Infinitives

| Verb | Example |
|---|---|
| agree | They agreed **to help**. |
| decide | She decided **to resign**. |
| expect | I expect **to arrive** by noon. |
| hope | We hope **to see** you soon. |
| learn | He learned **to code** in college. |
| need | You need **to rest**. |
| offer | She offered **to drive**. |
| plan | We plan **to visit** next summer. |
| promise | He promised **to call**. |
| refuse | She refused **to answer**. |
| seem | It seems **to be** working. |
| want | I want **to go**. |
| would like | I'd like **to order**. |

❌ She decided resigning.
❌ He wants going.

## Verbs That Take Both — With Change in Meaning

| Verb | Gerund Meaning | Infinitive Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **remember** | remember a past action | remember to do a future action |
| | ✅ I remember **locking** the door. *(I did it)* | ✅ Remember **to lock** the door. *(don't forget)* |
| **forget** | forget a past action | forget to do a future action |
| | ✅ I forgot **meeting** her. *(I met her, but forgot)* | ✅ I forgot **to meet** her. *(I didn't meet her)* |
| **stop** | cease an action | stop in order to do something |
| | ✅ She stopped **smoking**. *(quit)* | ✅ She stopped **to smoke**. *(paused to have a cigarette)* |
| **try** | experiment | attempt (but difficult) |
| | ✅ Try **restarting** the computer. *(test this solution)* | ✅ I tried **to lift** the box. *(couldn't)* |
| **regret** | regret a past action | regret to inform (formal) |
| | ✅ I regret **saying** that. *(I said it)* | ✅ I regret **to inform** you... *(I'm about to tell you)* |
| **mean** | involve / result in | intend |
| | ✅ This means **starting** over. *(it involves starting over)* | ✅ I meant **to call** you. *(I intended to call)* |

## Gerund After Prepositions

After a preposition, always use a gerund:

✅ She is interested **in learning**.
✅ He left **without saying** goodbye.
✅ Thank you **for helping**.
✅ I'm tired **of waiting**.
❌ She is interested in to learn.

## Infinitive of Purpose

Use an infinitive to explain why:

✅ I went to the store **to buy** milk.
✅ She saved money **to travel**.
❌ I went to the store for buying milk. *(not idiomatic)*
❌ I went to the store for to buy milk. *(doubly wrong)*

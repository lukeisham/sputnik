# Prepositions

Prepositions show relationships between nouns (or pronouns) and other elements in a sentence. They indicate location, time, direction, cause, manner, and other abstract relationships.

## Common Prepositions

### Location (Place)

| Preposition | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **at** | specific point | ✅ She is **at** the door. |
| **on** | surface | ✅ The book is **on** the table. |
| **in** | enclosed space | ✅ The keys are **in** my bag. |
| **above / over** | higher than | ✅ A plane flew **above** the clouds. |
| **below / under** | lower than | ✅ The cat is **under** the bed. |
| **next to / beside** | adjacent | ✅ Sit **next to** me. |
| **between** | two items | ✅ Share it **between** the two of you. |
| **among** | three or more | ✅ Share it **among** the group. |

### Time

| Preposition | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **at** | specific time | ✅ The train leaves **at** 3 PM. |
| **on** | day / date | ✅ See you **on** Monday. |
| **in** | month / year / season | ✅ It's cold **in** January. |
| **by** | deadline | ✅ Finish **by** Friday. |
| **for** | duration | ✅ We waited **for** an hour. |
| **since** | starting point | ✅ He's worked here **since** 2020. |
| **during** | within a period | ✅ She slept **during** the movie. |
| **until / till** | up to a point | ✅ Stay **until** I return. |

### Direction / Movement

✅ She walked **to** the store.
✅ He jumped **into** the pool.
✅ They ran **across** the bridge.
✅ The bird flew **through** the window.
✅ We drove **toward** the mountains.
✅ Water flowed **down** the pipe.

## Prepositional Phrases

A prepositional phrase = preposition + object (noun/noun phrase):

✅ **in the morning**
✅ **under the old oak tree**
✅ **with great enthusiasm**
✅ **despite the heavy rain**

### Function as Adjectives
✅ The book **on the shelf** is mine. *(modifies "book")*
✅ I need a chair **with armrests**. *(modifies "chair")*

### Function as Adverbs
✅ She arrived **after dinner**. *(modifies "arrived" — time)*
✅ He spoke **with confidence**. *(modifies "spoke" — manner)*
✅ They moved **toward the exit**. *(modifies "moved" — direction)*

## Common Preposition Mistakes

### Ending a Sentence with a Preposition

The old rule ("never end a sentence with a preposition") is **no longer enforced** in modern usage — but avoid it in formal writing:

✅ Who are you talking **to**? *(natural, modern)*
✅ **To** whom are you talking? *(formal, traditional)*
✅ This is the box I put it **in**. *(acceptable)*

### In vs Into

**In** = location (inside). **Into** = movement toward the inside.

✅ The fish is **in** the tank. *(location)*
✅ She jumped **into** the pool. *(movement)*
❌ She jumped **in** the pool. *(acceptable in casual speech, but "into" is more precise)*

### Of vs Have (Could Of / Would Of)

❌ I could **of** gone.
✅ I could **have** gone. *(or "could've")*
❌ You should **of** called.
✅ You should **have** called.

### Different From vs Different Than

✅ This is **different from** that. *(preferred in formal writing)*
✅ This is **different than** that. *(acceptable, especially US English)*

### Prepositions in Questions

❌ **Where** is the party **at**? *(redundant — "at" adds nothing)*
✅ **Where** is the party?

## Verbs + Prepositions (Dependent Prepositions)

Some verbs require specific prepositions:

| Verb + Prep | Example |
|---|---|
| depend **on** | ✅ It depends **on** the weather. |
| insist **on** | ✅ She insisted **on** paying. |
| belong **to** | ✅ This belongs **to** me. |
| wait **for** | ✅ I waited **for** the bus. |
| listen **to** | ✅ Listen **to** the instructions. |
| look **at** | ✅ Look **at** this photo. |
| apologize **for** | ✅ I apologize **for** the delay. |
| complain **about** | ✅ He complained **about** the noise. |
| care **about** | ✅ She cares **about** the environment. |
| agree **with** (a person) / **to** (a plan) | ✅ I agree **with** you. / I agree **to** the terms. |

❌ I depend **of** my parents.
❌ She insisted **in** paying.

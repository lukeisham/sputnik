# Verb Tense Consistency

Verb tense indicates when an action takes place: past, present, or future. Within a sentence or paragraph, verb tenses should remain consistent unless there is a logical reason to shift.

## Key Rules

1. **Stay in One Tense** — Do not switch between past and present within the same narrative without cause.
2. **Past Perfect for Earlier Events** — Use *had + past participle* when describing an event that happened before another past event.
3. **Present Tense for General Truths** — Facts and universal truths stay in present tense even in a past-tense narrative.
4. **Literary Present** — When discussing literature, use present tense (*Shakespeare writes...*).

✅ She walked to the store and bought milk.
❌ She walked to the store and buys milk.

✅ He had already left when I arrived.
❌ He already left when I had arrived.

✅ The study showed that water boils at 100°C.
❌ The study showed that water boiled at 100°C.

# Tone and Register

Tone is the writer's attitude toward the subject and audience. Register is the level of formality. Matching both to context is essential for effective communication.

## Register Levels

| Level | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Formal** | Academic papers, legal documents, official reports | "It is recommended that the committee convene at its earliest convenience." |
| **Standard** | Business communication, essays, journalism | "The committee should meet as soon as possible." |
| **Informal** | Personal emails, blogs, casual conversation | "Hey, the committee should get together soon." |
| **Colloquial** | Close friends, dialogue | "The committee needs to get its act together, like, yesterday." |

## Shifting Register Inappropriately

Mixing registers within a passage creates tonal whiplash:

❌ The defendant's culpability is incontrovertible, and yeah, that's pretty bad.
❌ We must optimize our synergies across verticals — basically, let's work together.
❌ The author posits a compelling argument, and it's super persuasive.

✅ The defendant's culpability is incontrovertible, and the consequences are severe.
✅ Cross-departmental collaboration will improve efficiency.
✅ The author's argument is compelling and well-supported by evidence.

## Common Register Mistakes

| Error | Correction |
|---|---|
| Contractions in formal writing | ❌ The results don't support... → ✅ The results do not support... |
| Slang in professional contexts | ❌ That presentation was lit. → ✅ That presentation was excellent. |
| Overly stiff language in casual contexts | ❌ I would be most gratified if you could attend. → ✅ I'd love it if you could come. |
| Jargon with non-specialist audiences | ❌ We need to leverage our core competencies. → ✅ We need to focus on what we do best. |
| Text-speak in writing | ❌ btw, lmk if u can make it → ✅ By the way, let me know if you can make it. |

## Matching Tone to Purpose

| Purpose | Appropriate Tone |
|---|---|
| Persuade | Confident, reasonable, empathetic |
| Inform | Neutral, clear, objective |
| Entertain | Playful, vivid, engaging |
| Instruct | Clear, patient, encouraging |
| Criticize | Specific, constructive, fair |
| Apologize | Sincere, direct, accountable |

## Active Tone vs Passive Tone

Beyond active/passive voice, *tone* can be active or passive:

**Active tone:** Takes responsibility, names subjects, uses direct language.
✅ I made a mistake.
✅ The team decided to delay the launch.

**Passive tone:** Deflects responsibility, hides the actor, uses hedging.
❌ Mistakes were made.
❌ It was decided that the launch would be delayed.

## Hedging Language

Hedging weakens writing. Use it sparingly — only when genuine uncertainty exists.

| Weak (Over-Hedged) | Strong |
|---|---|
| It seems that perhaps we might want to consider... | I recommend... |
| I think it's kind of important to note... | Note that... |
| The data sort of suggests maybe... | The data suggests... / The data does not conclusively show... |

## Inclusive Tone

✅ Use gender-neutral language when gender is unknown (*they*, not *he*).
✅ Avoid idioms that assume shared cultural background (*hit a home run*, *sticky wicket*).
✅ Use plain language where possible — technical terms should be explained on first use.
✅ Frame to the reader's perspective: *"You'll notice..."* not *"One will observe..."*

✅ The report outlines three areas for improvement.
❌ As per my last email, I literally cannot even with these quarterly numbers.

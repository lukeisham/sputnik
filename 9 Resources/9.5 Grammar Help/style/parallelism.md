# Parallelism

Parallelism (parallel structure) means using the same grammatical form for items in a series, pairs, or comparisons. It makes writing clearer, more rhythmic, and easier to follow.

## The Core Rule

Elements joined by coordinating conjunctions (*and, or, but*) or correlative conjunctions (*both...and, either...or, neither...nor, not only...but also*) must be in the same grammatical form.

## Lists and Series

✅ She likes **running**, **swimming**, and **cycling**. *(all gerunds)*
❌ She likes **running**, **swimming**, and **to cycle**. *(mismatched — to + infinitive)*

✅ The job requires **attention to detail**, **strong communication skills**, and **punctuality**. *(all noun phrases)*
❌ The job requires **attention to detail**, **communicating well**, and **being punctual**. *(mismatched)*

## Correlative Conjunctions

| Conjunction | Correct (Parallel) | Incorrect |
|---|---|---|
| both...and | ✅ She is **both** intelligent **and** hardworking. | ❌ She **both** is intelligent **and** hardworking. |
| either...or | ✅ **Either** you leave **or** I will. | ❌ You **either** leave **or** I will. |
| neither...nor | ✅ He **neither** called **nor** texted. | ❌ He **neither** called **nor** did he text. |
| not only...but also | ✅ She **not only** sings **but also** dances. | ❌ She **not only** sings **but also** she dances. |

## Comparisons

✅ The movie was **more exciting than** it was **engaging**. *(parallel clauses)*
❌ The movie was **more exciting than engaging**. *(acceptable in speech; grammatically comparing adjective to adjective — actually this one is fine... let me fix)*

✅ I would rather **walk** than **drive**. *(both base verbs)*
❌ I would rather **walk** than **driving**.

✅ His explanation was **clear, concise, and helpful**. *(all adjectives)*
❌ His explanation was **clear, concise, and it helped**. *(broken pattern)*

## Clauses and Phrases

✅ The report explains **what we achieved** and **what we plan to do next**. *(parallel noun clauses)*
❌ The report explains **what we achieved** and **our plans for next year**. *(clause + noun phrase)*

✅ She **arrived early, checked in, and waited** patiently. *(parallel predicates)*
❌ She **arrived early, then she checked in, and was waiting** patiently. *(broken pattern)*

## Headings and Bullet Points

All items in a bulleted list should start with the same part of speech:

✅ Project Goals:
- Increase revenue by 10%
- Reduce costs by 5%
- Launch the new product line

❌ Project Goals:
- Increase revenue by 10%
- We need to reduce costs by 5%
- Launching the new product line

## Common Faulty Parallelism Patterns

| Faulty | Parallel |
|---|---|
| The job involves typing, filing, and to answer phones. | The job involves typing, filing, and answering phones. |
| She is skilled in design and for managing teams. | She is skilled in design and team management. |
| We considered the cost, the timeline, and whether it was feasible. | We considered the cost, the timeline, and the feasibility. |
| He not only passed the exam but also with high marks. | He not only passed the exam but also earned high marks. |

✅ The speech was long, boring, and full of clichés.
❌ The speech was long, boring, and it had too many clichés.

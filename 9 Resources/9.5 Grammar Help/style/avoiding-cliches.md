# Avoiding Clichés

A cliché is an overused phrase or expression that has lost its original impact. Relying on clichés makes writing feel lazy and unoriginal.

## Why Avoid Clichés?

- They weaken your voice — readers skim past them.
- They signal shallow thinking — you're borrowing someone else's phrasing.
- They date your writing — today's buzzword is tomorrow's cliché.

## Common Clichés by Category

### Business
| Cliché | Alternative |
|---|---|
| think outside the box | approach creatively, consider new angles |
| low-hanging fruit | easy wins, quick gains |
| move the needle | make measurable progress |
| circle back | follow up, revisit |
| at the end of the day | ultimately, in the end |
| synergy | collaboration, combined effort |
| core competency | key strength, primary expertise |
| disrupt the industry | fundamentally change, reshape |
| best-in-class | excellent, leading |
| deep dive | examine in detail, thoroughly analyze |

### Academic / Formal
| Cliché | Alternative |
|---|---|
| in today's society | now, currently |
| since the dawn of time | throughout history |
| in this day and age | today, currently |
| a double-edged sword | both beneficial and harmful |
| last but not least | finally |

### General
| Cliché | Alternative |
|---|---|

| actions speak louder than words | deeds matter more than promises |

## How to Spot and Fix Clichés

### 1. Read Aloud
If a phrase rolls off the tongue too easily, it's probably a cliché. Fresh writing has a slight friction — it makes you pause.

### 2. Ask: "What Do I Actually Mean?"
❌ We need to think outside the box.
→ What does "outside the box" look like? Unconventional pricing? A new distribution channel?
✅ We should explore direct-to-consumer sales rather than relying on retail partners.

### 3. Replace with Concrete Detail
❌ She was as busy as a bee.
✅ She was juggling three client projects, a board presentation, and a product launch.

### 4. Use Fresh Metaphors
❌ The startup was burning the candle at both ends.
✅ The startup was running on caffeine and conviction.

## When Is a Cliché Acceptable?

- **Dialogue:** Real people use clichés. A character who speaks in clichés can be intentional characterization.
- **Headlines / Titles:** A twist on a cliché can be effective: *"Think Inside the Box"* or *"A Bite the Bullet Moment"*
- **First drafts:** It's fine to write clichés and replace them during revision. Don't let cliché-hunting block your flow.

## Verdict

One cliché in 5,000 words won't ruin your writing. But a density of one per paragraph makes readers check out. The goal is not to eliminate every cliché — it's to make your default setting "fresh" rather than "familiar."

✅ We need an unconventional approach to this problem.
❌ We need to think outside the box.
✅ Ultimately, trust depends on honest communication.
❌ At the end of the day, it is what it is.

# Getting Started with ASCII Art

ASCII art is the craft of composing pictures, diagrams, and decorative elements using only the printable characters available in the standard ASCII and extended Unicode character sets — letters, numbers, punctuation, and line-drawing glyphs. In Sputnik, ASCII art is a first-class medium: you can browse a curated library of reusable pieces, preview them in real time, and insert them directly into Markdown documents, code comments, or plain-text notes.

## The Monospaced Editor

ASCII art depends on a fixed-width grid where every character occupies the same amount of horizontal space. Sputnik's editor includes a dedicated **monospaced mode** that enforces a uniform character cell width, making it easy to align box edges, columns, and geometric shapes. Toggle monospaced mode anytime from the editor toolbar or with `Cmd+Shift+M`. While in this mode, the editor also displays a subtle column ruler every 10 characters so you can keep your art consistently sized without counting characters manually.

## The ASCII Library Browser

The ASCII Library — accessible from the toolbar or via `Cmd+Shift+L` — organises hundreds of reusable pieces into five categories: **Arrows**, **Decorative**, **Dividers**, **Frames**, and **Symbols**. Each piece has a short description, search tags, and a live preview panel. You can filter by category, type a keyword, or browse the full collection in a scrollable grid. When you find a piece you like, click it once to see a magnified preview, then click **Insert** to place it at the cursor in the active document.

## Preview Before You Insert

Every library piece shows a **live preview** rendered in the same monospaced font your editor uses, so what you see in the browser is exactly what will appear in your document. If the piece contains multi-line art (such as a framed box), the preview panel expands vertically to show the full shape. This WYSIWYG approach eliminates the guesswork of "how wide is this divider?" or "will this box fit my text?"

## Basic Formatting Concepts

Successful ASCII art comes down to three principles: **alignment**, **proportionality**, and **readability**. Always start on a blank line and keep your art left-aligned unless you're deliberately centering it. Use the column ruler to verify that vertical edges line up. When combining multiple pieces — for example, placing a heart symbol above a divider — check that their widths are compatible. Finally, leave at least one blank line before and after your art so it doesn't collide with surrounding Markdown content.

## Quick-Start Checklist

1. Enable **monospaced mode** (`Cmd+Shift+M`).
2. Open the **ASCII Library** (`Cmd+Shift+L`).
3. Browse or search for a piece; click to preview.
4. Click **Insert** or drag the piece into your document.
5. Adjust surrounding whitespace so the art breathes.
6. Toggle monospaced mode off when you're done to return to proportional editing.

Start with simple dividers and symbols, then graduate to framed boxes and multi-element compositions as you build confidence. The library is always there when you need inspiration.
